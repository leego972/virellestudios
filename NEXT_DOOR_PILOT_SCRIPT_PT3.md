# NEXT DOOR: PILOT SCRIPT (PART 3)

## SCENE 7: NINA AND CELESTE PRIVATE SCENE (8:45 PM)
**Scene Description:** The Vale kitchen. Celeste is pouring a second bottle of wine. Nina has followed her in to help, but really to assess her away from Marcus.
**Emotional Objective:** Celeste tries to charm and dominate Nina. Nina refuses to be charmed, establishing herself as Celeste’s intellectual equal.
**Subtext:** Celeste is looking for the cracks in Nina’s marriage. Nina is showing Celeste that she sees exactly what she is doing.
**Performance Tone:** Velvet hostility. Two highly intelligent women circling each other.

**CELESTE**
(Pouring the wine, her voice low and intimate)
You know, when we first moved here, I hated it. The silence. It feels like everyone is just waiting for someone else to make a mistake so they have something to talk about.

**NINA**
(Leaning against the counter, composed)
I imagine that’s true of most places with this kind of property tax.

**CELESTE**
(Laughs softly, handing Nina a glass)
Exactly. But Daniel... he seems like a good man. Very earnest. He must be under a lot of pressure right now. The logistics business is brutal.

**NINA**
(Taking the glass, not drinking)
He handles pressure well. He’s very organized.

**CELESTE**
(Taking a slow sip, her eyes never leaving Nina’s)
Is he? That’s good. Marcus always says the organized ones break the hardest when the system fails. Because they don't know how to improvise. 

**NINA**
(A tight, cold smile)
That’s a very cynical way to look at the world, Celeste.

**CELESTE**
(Softly, stepping slightly closer)
It’s a very safe way to look at the world, Nina. I just want you to know... if things get complicated, if Daniel’s organization starts to fail... you can come to me. We take care of our friends.

**NINA**
(Setting the wine glass down, her voice dropping a register)
I appreciate the offer. But my husband and I don't require taking care of. We handle our own messes.

**What Changes:** Celeste realizes Nina is not a weak, panicked suburban wife. Nina realizes Celeste is actively probing their marriage for a vulnerability to exploit. The battle lines are drawn.

## SCENE 8: THE SMALL FAVOR (9:15 PM)
**Scene Description:** The Vale driveway. Daniel and Nina are leaving. Marcus walks them out. He stops Daniel near his car.
**Emotional Objective:** Marcus asserts dominance by making a request Daniel cannot refuse without looking suspicious.
**Subtext:** Marcus knows Daniel is hiding something. He is testing how far Daniel will bend to avoid drawing attention.
**Performance Tone:** Quietly intimidating, dryly funny, but absolutely unyielding.

**MARCUS**
(Hands in his pockets, perfectly relaxed)
Daniel. Before you go. I need a small favor.

**DANIEL**
(Already defensive, trying to sound casual)
Sure. What is it?

**MARCUS**
(Looking at Daniel’s SUV)
My brother-in-law is flying in tomorrow night. Needs a pickup from the private airfield out by Route 9. My driver is out sick, and my car is in the shop. You’ve got the space. 

**DANIEL**
(Panic flaring in his eyes)
Tomorrow night? I’m... I have a lot of unpacking to do. And it’s a long drive.

**MARCUS**
(A dry, sparse smile)
It’s an hour each way. He’s got some heavy bags. You clearly know how to pack a trunk, Daniel. I saw you out here at four in the morning making sure it was spotless.

Daniel freezes. The blood drains from his face. Marcus’s voice remains entirely conversational.

**MARCUS**
(Stepping closer, his voice dropping)
It’s just a ride, Daniel. A neighborly favor. You’ll pick him up at midnight. He’ll give you the address. 

**DANIEL**
(Trapped, swallowing hard)
Midnight. Okay. Yeah, I can do that.

**MARCUS**
(Patting Daniel on the shoulder, a little too hard)
I appreciate it. Sleep well, Daniel. You look like you need it.

**What Changes:** Daniel is officially compromised. He is now performing a task for a criminal organization simply to prevent them from looking closer at his own crime.

## SCENE 9: THE DETECTIVES ARRIVE (11:00 PM)
**Scene Description:** The Cross living room. Daniel is pacing. Nina is sitting on the couch, watching him. The doorbell rings. It’s late. Too late for neighbors. Daniel opens the door. DETECTIVE NATHAN COLE and DETECTIVE LENA MERCER are standing on the porch.
**Emotional Objective:** The sudden, terrifying intrusion of the law. The detectives are not aggressive; they are deceptively calm.
**Subtext:** Cole and Mercer already suspect Daniel. They are just watching him react.
**Performance Tone:** Dry, measured, probing. The pressure of the state colliding with suburban panic.

**COLE**
(Holding up a badge, his voice entirely flat)
Mr. Cross? Detective Cole. This is Detective Mercer. Apologies for the hour.

**DANIEL**
(Voice shaking slightly)
Is something wrong? Did something happen in the neighborhood?

**MERCER**
(Precise, quietly probing)
Not in the neighborhood, no. We’re actually investigating an incident at a warehouse facility on the south side. Your company’s name came up on a manifest.

**NINA**
(Stepping up beside Daniel, her voice perfectly controlled)
My husband’s company handles logistics for hundreds of warehouses, Detectives. Is there a specific shipment you’re looking for?

**COLE**
(Looking at Nina, assessing her instantly)
A specific vehicle, actually. A dark SUV. Like the one in your driveway. 

**DANIEL**
(Over-explaining, panicked)
That’s a very common car. Half the street drives one.

**MERCER**
(A small, empathetic smile that is entirely fake)
It is common. But not many of them smell like industrial bleach from the sidewalk, Mr. Cross.

Cole doesn't smile. He just stares at Daniel.

**COLE**
(Dry, deceptively simple)
We’re just doing the rounds tonight. Eliminating variables. We’ll likely need to speak with you tomorrow when you’re less... tired. Have a good night, folks.

The detectives turn and walk away. Daniel closes the door, leaning his forehead against the wood, completely broken. Nina stares at his back.

**What Changes:** The pilot ends. The audience knows the Vales are manipulating Daniel, Mavis is watching him, and the police are already at his door. There is no escape. The trap is closed.
