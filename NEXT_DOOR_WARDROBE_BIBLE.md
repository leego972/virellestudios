# NEXT DOOR: LOCKED WARDROBE BIBLE (PILOT)

## PART 1: CHARACTER WARDROBE IDENTITIES

Wardrobe in *Next Door* is not decoration. It is a storytelling system. It communicates class, family identity, emotional state, control versus unraveling, seduction versus safety, and the boundary between the domestic and criminal worlds.

### 1. Marcus Vale
**Core Identity:** Wealthy Eastern European patriarch. Quiet luxury, disciplined, severe, and expensive without showing off. He should always look like a man who has already decided what happens next.
**Silhouette:** Clean shoulders, straight lines, precise fit. No visual looseness unless intentionally domestic.
**Palette:** Charcoal, black, midnight navy, dark olive, cold stone, deep espresso.
**Fabrics:** Cashmere, brushed wool, fine merino, matte cotton, smooth leather accents.
**Homewear:** Fitted dark knit polo or fine-gauge sweater, tailored trousers, dark indoor loafers or minimal leather slippers.
**Social Wear:** Open-collar dark shirt or knit polo under a soft tailored jacket. Minimal watch. No shiny suit energy, no loud belt buckles, no visible logos.
**Never Wear:** Trendy streetwear, bright colors, loud prints, flashy gangster stereotypes, sports jerseys, cheap shine.

### 2. Celeste Vale
**Core Identity:** Eastern European luxury. Composed, predatory, immaculate. Sensual through precision, not exposure. She should look like hospitality was weaponized.
**Silhouette:** Sculpted, elegant, long clean lines. Fitted but never vulgar. Strong waist and shoulder control.
**Palette:** Ivory, black, champagne, deep burgundy, emerald, slate blue, cool taupe.
**Fabrics:** Silk, satin, crepe, cashmere, fine wool, smooth leather. Controlled shine only.
**Homewear:** Silk blouse with tailored trousers, elevated knit dress, expensive robe or lounge set in neutral tones. Always polished enough to receive someone.
**Social Wear:** Architectural dress or silk blouse with cigarette trousers. Heels or elegant pointed flats. Structured trench or long coat. Very deliberate jewelry.
**Accessories:** Thin gold or platinum pieces. One strong ring or earrings, never clutter. Watch only if extremely elegant and minimal.
**Never Wear:** Messy hair, athleisure, loud florals, suburban mom casual, anything "cute."

### 3. Adrian Vale
**Core Identity:** Dangerous charm. Younger bloodline luxury, relaxed elegance, human temptation. Slightly more modern than Marcus. He should look like trouble entered the room smiling.
**Silhouette:** Soft tailoring, fluid movement, open collar, elegant ease. Sensual looseness without sloppiness.
**Palette:** Cream, navy, black, espresso, burgundy, dark bronze, muted gold accents.
**Fabrics:** Silk-blend shirts, fine cotton, open-weave knits, soft wool, light tailoring.
**Homewear:** Expensive casual shirt partially unbuttoned, slim dark trousers, loafers or boots. Sleeves occasionally rolled.
**Social Wear:** Soft blazer or open jacket, shirt with first buttons open. Polished but never stiff. One chain or understated ring allowed.
**Never Wear:** Hoodies, techwear, novelty fashion, sloppy sneakers, cheap denim.

### 4. Jaden Vale
**Core Identity:** Expensive, guarded teen. Privileged but watched. Understated, not trying too hard, slightly detached. He should look like money without enthusiasm.
**Silhouette:** Clean teen luxury. Fitted but relaxed. Darker youth palette, minimal branding.
**Palette:** Black, washed charcoal, muted green, faded navy, off-white, steel grey.
**Fabrics:** Premium hoodies, good denim, matte bomber jacket, structured overshirts, premium tees.
**Footwear:** Clean expensive sneakers. Nothing loud.
**Never Wear:** Childish graphics, neon, busy sportswear, clownish hypebeast styling.

### 5. Daniel Cross
**Core Identity:** Working man. Proud, useful, financially stretched. Trying to look stable. He should always look one bill away from embarrassment.
**Silhouette:** Sturdy, functional, slightly worn. Not sloppy by choice, but visibly under pressure.
**Palette:** Faded blue, work grey, tan, olive, brown, off-white, washed black.
**Fabrics:** Denim, cotton, work shirts, henleys, old hoodie, weathered canvas jacket.
**Homewear:** Jeans, worn tee or henley. Socks on hardwood. Old sneakers or work boots near the door.
**Public/Work Wear:** Route supervisor shirt, utility jacket, affordable watch, practical boots.
**Never Wear:** Sleek luxury tailoring, highly fitted designer clothes, bright fashion colors.

### 6. Nina Cross
**Core Identity:** Practical intelligence. Budget-conscious elegance. Grounded, emotionally controlled, attractive without trying to weaponize it. She should look like a woman who keeps the house from collapsing quietly.
**Silhouette:** Clean, modest, functional, quietly flattering. More structure when protecting herself.
**Palette:** Cream, navy, soft taupe, dusty rose, muted green, charcoal, faded plum.
**Fabrics:** Knitwear, cotton poplin, soft structured trousers, practical dresses, simple blouses.
**Accessories:** Small earrings, simple chain, wedding ring, maybe one practical watch.
**Homewear:** Soft sweater, well-worn but nice jeans or trousers, house flats or barefoot, practical cardigan.
**Social Wear:** Blouse with clean trousers, simple but flattering dress, affordable but tasteful jacket, minimal jewelry.
**Never Wear:** Loud glamour, skin-tight seduction looks, chaotic prints, obvious fast-fashion trend pieces.

### 7. Mia Cross
**Core Identity:** Teen realism. Stubborn, active, emotionally direct, low-maintenance. She should feel like a real teenager, not a fashion campaign.
**Silhouette:** Functional, mobile, slightly messy. Believable teen layering.
**Palette:** Washed denim, muted red, charcoal, forest green, white, faded black.
**Pieces:** Hoodie, cargo pants, denim shorts or jeans, sneakers, oversized tee, flannel or light zip hoodie.
**Never Wear:** Hyper-glam teen styling, perfect influencer outfits, adult-coded luxury looks.

### 8. Mavis Whitlock
**Core Identity:** Widow observer. Old-fashioned but alert. Harmless-looking surveillance system. Funny and territorial. She should look harmless enough that people forget she is always watching.
**Silhouette:** Practical older-lady shapes. Floral or patterned housewear, cardigans, nightgown, robe, apron, porch layers.
**Palette:** Faded floral tones, mauve, pale blue, cream, washed green, soft burgundy.
**Pieces:** Floral nightgown, cardigan, orthopedic but believable older-lady footwear, house slippers. Reading glasses on chain if useful.
**Never Wear:** Polished elegance, aristocratic severity, anything that removes her neighborhood texture.

### 9. Detective Nathan Cole
**Core Identity:** Experienced major-crimes detective. Dry, unimpressed, functional authority. He should look like someone who has been in too many living rooms at bad hours.
**Silhouette:** Plain structured jacket, dark shirt. No flashy police TV styling. Practical, worn professionalism.
**Palette:** Navy, charcoal, black, white shirt occasionally, muted brown leather details.
**Pieces:** Field jacket or blazer, dark trousers, plain boots or shoes. Badge, notebook, minimal watch.
**Never Wear:** Action-hero tactical cosplay, too much visible police gear, designer detective nonsense.

### 10. Detective Lena Mercer
**Core Identity:** Precise, emotionally surgical, intelligent. Low-key elegant authority. She should look composed enough that people tell her more than they mean to.
**Silhouette:** Clean lines, fitted but restrained. Polished without vanity.
**Palette:** Black, navy, slate, ivory, muted steel blue.
**Pieces:** Fitted jacket, blouse or fine knit, trousers, boots or low heels depending on scene. Small but sharp accessories.
**Never Wear:** Glam detective styling, overdone femininity, loud colors.

### 11. Viktor Saric
**Core Identity:** Operational spine. Insomnia-worn. Dark utilitarian realism. Prepared to discard clothes after work. He should look like the part of the operation nobody wants to meet after midnight.
**Silhouette:** Dark, functional, unmarked, efficient.
**Palette:** Black, dark grey, oil green, faded navy.
**Pieces:** Dark coat, plain knit or tee, durable trousers, practical boots. Cheap digital watch worn on the inside of the left wrist.
**Never Wear:** Suburban luxury, bright color, tailored glamour, street-thug cliché.

## PART 2: PILOT SCENE-BY-SCENE WARDROBE ASSIGNMENTS

**Scene 1 — The Trunk (4:30 AM)**
* Daniel: Old dark hoodie over tee, work pants or worn jeans, practical shoes with bleach/cleaner risk. Sleeves pushed up. Damp from panic-cleaning. He looks compromised and sleepless.
* Mavis: Floral nightgown, cardigan or robe, slippers. Mug as accessory. She looks absurdly domestic and therefore more dangerous.

**Scene 2 — The Kitchen (6:30 AM)**
* Nina: Practical early-morning knit top or sweater, simple house trousers/jeans. Hair tied back or clipped. No makeup-heavy look. She represents control.
* Daniel: Changed shirt but still rough. Same fatigue in body. He represents unraveling.

**Scene 3 — The Welcome at the Door (10:00 AM)**
* Nina: Simple daytime blouse or knit top, clean but inexpensive home trousers. Slightly tired face.
* Daniel: Casual home repair/move-in clothing. Maybe rolled sleeves, trying to seem approachable.
* Celeste: Immaculate daytime luxury look. Ivory or champagne silk blouse with tailored trousers. Elegant low heel. Minimal jewelry.
* Marcus: Dark knit polo or fine-gauge sweater. Tailored dark trousers. Expensive watch minimally visible.
* Adrian: Open-collar refined casual. Cream or deep navy shirt. Relaxed expensive trousers.
* Jaden: Understated premium teen basics.
* *Goal:* The Vales must look like a magazine cover against the Cross family’s move-in fatigue.

**Scene 4 — Mia and Jaden (4:00 PM)**
* Mia: Teen hoodie or tee, cargo pants or jeans, sneakers. Believable physicality.
* Jaden: Dark premium hoodie or bomber, clean sneakers. Phone in hand.
* *Goal:* They should feel age-appropriate and natural, not overstyled.

**Scene 5 — Mavis Intrudes (6:45 PM)**
* Mavis: Cardigan over housedress or daytime housewear. Casserole dish prop.
* Nina: Changed into dinner-prep outfit. Simple flattering blouse or practical dress.
* Daniel: Trying too hard to dress up. Button-down that does not fully sit right.
* *Goal:* The Cross family should look like they are trying to rise to the occasion, not naturally living in it.

**Scene 6 — The Vale Dinner (8:00 PM)**
* Marcus: Dark open-collar shirt or refined knit with tailored trousers. No jacket if too formal, but still powerful.
* Celeste: Evening house-hostess luxury. Silk or structured dress in burgundy, emerald, or black. Heels. Hair perfect.
* Adrian: Sensual polished evening casual. Dark shirt, first buttons open. Elegant trousers and loafers.
* Jaden: Upscale teen look, slightly dressed for dinner.
* Daniel: Best affordable dinner shirt. Maybe jacket removed or no tie. Trying too hard but not ridiculous.
* Nina: Clean, tasteful dinner dress or blouse/trouser combination. Understated but attractive.
* Mia: Slightly improved teen outfit but still authentic.
* *Goal:* Visually communicate class seduction. The Cross family should look decent but outclassed. The Vales should look effortless.

**Scene 7 — Nina and Celeste Private Scene (8:45 PM)**
* Nina: Same dinner look. Functional elegance.
* Celeste: Same dinner look but seen more closely. Controlled luxury power.

**Scene 8 — The Small Favor (9:15 PM)**
* Marcus: Dark outer layer added if night air. More severe silhouette. He looks unchanged by the night.
* Daniel: Leaving outfit from dinner, slightly disordered by nerves. He looks worn by one conversation.

**Scene 9 — The Garage Reveal (9:30 PM)**
* Mia: Teen dinner-lite outfit, possibly with a hoodie thrown on.
* Jaden: Dark understated youth luxury.
* Marcus: Still controlled.
* Adrian: Loosened slightly but still polished.
* Viktor: Dark operational wear.
* *Goal:* Contrast "family dinner" with "crime machinery."

**Scene 10 — The Kitchen Confrontation (10:00 PM)**
* Nina: Shoes off. Cardigan added or evening look slightly softened.
* Daniel: Shirt loosened, sleeves rolled, tie absent if any.
* *Goal:* Show emotional comedown and stress.

**Scene 11 — The Detectives Arrive (11:00 PM)**
* Cole: Dark detective field jacket, plain shirt, practical shoes.
* Mercer: Fitted coat/jacket, blouse or fine knit, trousers, boots.
* *Goal:* Cut through the suburban night and feel like reality arriving.

**Scene 12 — The Porch Watch (11:43 PM)**
* Mavis: Robe, cardigan, or porch nightwear. Notebook prop. Full neighborhood surveillance energy.

**Scene 13 — The Pickup (Midnight)**
* Daniel: Same late-night worn clothes. Visual fatigue.
* Viktor: Dark coat, plain dark knit or tee, durable trousers, boots. Inside-wrist digital watch visible in at least one key shot.
* *Goal:* Viktor must look like a man from a different show just entered this one.

**Scene 14 — The End of the Night (3:00 AM)**
* Marcus: Same composed dark clothing. Maybe coat removed, making the intrusion feel worse. He looks more at home in Daniel's house than Daniel does.
* Daniel: Same wrung-out post-airfield look.

## PART 3: CONTINUITY RULES

1. Do not randomly change hair, jewelry, or color palette between scenes.
2. Cross family wardrobe must show repetition and budget realism.
3. Vale family wardrobe must show controlled luxury and intentional variation.
4. Jaden and Mia must remain youth-realistic.
5. Mavis must remain visually consistent as neighborhood surveillance domesticity.
6. Detectives must remain clean, grounded, procedural.
7. Viktor must remain function-first dark utilitarian.
8. Any wardrobe shift must reflect:
   * Time of day
   * Social setting
   * Temperature
   * Emotional shift
   * Practical continuity

## PART 5: VIRELLE GENERATION CONSISTENCY NOTES

To prevent AI hallucination drift inside Virelle generation:
1. **Lock the Palettes:** Use negative prompts to exclude bright colors, neons, and loud prints for all characters except Mia's occasional muted reds.
2. **Lock the Silhouettes:** Use explicit tailoring terms for the Vales (e.g., "structured shoulders," "clean lines," "silk drape") and explicit texture terms for the Crosses (e.g., "worn denim," "faded cotton," "practical knit").
3. **Control the Accessories:** Limit generated jewelry. Explicitly prompt "minimal jewelry" for Celeste and Nina. Explicitly prompt "cheap digital watch on inside of left wrist" for Viktor in every generation.
4. **Anchor Mavis:** Always generate Mavis with "faded floral patterns" and "layered cardigans" to prevent her from looking too polished.
5. **Anchor the Detectives:** Use "unmarked dark field jacket" and "plain tailored coat" to avoid stereotypical police uniforms or badges unless explicitly required.

## PART 6: SASHA DUO (VALE SECURITY) WARDROBE BIBLE

The Sasha Duo operates as the polished, visible security layer of the Vale household. Their wardrobe is dark, functional, and highly controlled, but with a subtle split that reflects their operational roles.

### Big Sasha (The Hard Edge)
**Core Identity:** Imposing, stationary threat. Heavy, dense, weathered.
**Silhouette:** Broad, thick, durable. Utilitarian shapes that dominate a doorway.
**Palette:** Black, charcoal, dark olive, faded navy, steel grey.
**Pieces:** Heavy overshirts, dark fitted tees, cargo/utility trousers, thick leather belt, sturdy black boots.
**Visual Habit:** Right sleeve rolled to the elbow when paying close attention.
**Never Wear:** Logos, obvious body armor, trendy streetwear, anything that looks new.

### Little Sasha (The Smooth Contact)
**Core Identity:** Deceptively approachable, mobile, socially intelligent.
**Silhouette:** Leaner, quicker, slightly younger energy. Clean and functional.
**Palette:** Black, slate, dark green, washed charcoal, muted navy.
**Pieces:** Lighter bombers or overshirts, fitted dark henleys, slim utility trousers, clean dark boots or dark trainers.
**Visual Habit:** Always holding a small notebook or phone at the gate.
**Never Wear:** Heavy tactical gear, bright colors, sloppy fits.

## PART 7: HADDAD FAMILY WARDROBE BIBLE

The Haddads are the second criminal power in *Next Door*. Their wardrobe is expansive, warm, luxurious, and completely unapologetic. They do not hide their wealth behind minimalism like the Vales.

### Tariq Haddad (The Patriarch)
**Core Identity:** Charismatic, unpredictable power. Expansive luxury.
**Silhouette:** Relaxed but impeccably tailored. Comfortable dominance.
**Palette:** Deep burgundy, navy, rich chocolate brown, gold accents.
**Pieces:** Cashmere sweaters, velvet jackets, heavy silk shirts with open collars.
**Accessories:** Heavy gold watch, signet ring. Visible but not cheap.
**Never Wear:** Stiff formal suits, tight clothing, cheap shine.

### Yasmine Haddad (The Matriarch)
**Core Identity:** Overwhelming hospitality, fierce protection. Takes up space.
**Silhouette:** Glamorous, bold, structured drape.
**Palette:** Emerald green, ruby red, deep saffron, black with gold threading.
**Pieces:** Heavy satin blouses, embroidered layers, rich dresses.
**Accessories:** Layered gold necklaces, statement earrings, heavy bracelets.
**Never Wear:** Minimalist neutral tones, casual athleisure, anything subtle.

### Zayn Haddad (The Heir Apparent)
**Core Identity:** Ambitious, reckless, flashy. Wants to modernize the operation.
**Silhouette:** Sharp, modern, aggressive streetwear mixed with tailoring.
**Palette:** Black, stark white, oxblood, metallic accents.
**Pieces:** Designer leather jackets, high-end cotton tees, premium streetwear trousers.
**Accessories:** Flashy designer watch, modern chains.
**Never Wear:** Traditional old-world suits, understated basics.
