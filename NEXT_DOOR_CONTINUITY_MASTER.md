## 3. WARDROBE ABSOLUTES
- **The Sasha Rule:** Big Sasha and Little Sasha wear polished executive-protection suits at all times by default. The only exception is when doing dirty operational labor (e.g., in the garage or airfield), where the jacket comes off and a ribbed undershirt/wifebeater is permitted. No henleys, no cargo pants, no tactical gear, no generic henchman outfits.
- **The Vale Rule:** Expensive, tailored, neutral colors. Cashmere, silk, fine wool. Nothing loud.
- **The Cross Rule:** Practical, slightly worn, accessible. Denim, cotton, fleece. Nothing that looks perfectly tailored.
- **The Haddad Rule:** Maximalist, warm, expensive. Gold jewelry, deep reds, rich textures.

## 4. SET LOGIC ABSOLUTES
- **Cross House:** Cluttered, warm, lived-in, slightly cramped. The lighting is practical and sometimes unflattering.
- **Vale House:** Sterile, massive, perfectly curated, intimidating. The lighting is either gallery-perfect (day) or surgical/architectural (night).
- **The Garage Seam:** The Vale garage is not a messy workshop; it is a clinically clean, bright white operating theater for criminal logistics.
- **The Porch:** Mavis's domain. It is a watchtower, not just a seating area.

## 5. PLOT DEPENDENCIES
- The police who arrive in Scene 11 are generic, unnamed detectives. They are NOT Mercer and Hayes, and they are definitely NOT Viktor.
- Daniel's descent is driven by Marcus's "small favor" (the briefcase), not by a pre-existing criminal connection.
- The tension comes from the proximity of the two houses, not from external action set pieces.
