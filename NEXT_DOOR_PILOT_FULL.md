# NEXT DOOR: PILOT SCRIPT (PART 1)

## SCENE 1: THE TRUNK (4:30 AM)
**Scene Description:** The Cross driveway. Pitch black. DANIEL CROSS is aggressively scrubbing the trunk of his SUV with a heavy-duty sponge and industrial cleaner. His hands are shaking. He drops the sponge. He forces himself to breathe, picks it up, and resumes. Across the street, a porch light flicks on. MAVIS WHITLOCK steps out in a floral nightgown, holding a mug.
**Emotional Objective:** Establish Daniel’s absolute terror and Mavis’s baseline nosiness.
**Subtext:** Daniel thinks he’s alone. He isn’t. Mavis doesn’t know what he’s doing, but she knows it’s wrong.
**Performance Tone:** Silent panic colliding with cheerful, invasive neighborliness.

**MAVIS**
(Calling out, voice carrying too far in the quiet)
Bit early for spring cleaning, isn't it, Daniel?

Daniel freezes. He slowly turns around. He tries to arrange his face into a normal, neighborly expression. It looks like a hostage video.

**DANIEL**
(Voice tight, slightly too loud)
Mavis. Hi. Yeah, just... spilled some fertilizer. Didn't want it to set into the carpet.

**MAVIS**
(Takes a slow sip from her mug)
Fertilizer. At four in the morning.

**DANIEL**
Couldn't sleep.

**MAVIS**
(Smiling, entirely unbothered by his panic)
Well, you make sure you get the edges. That stuff eats right through the lining. Ruined my Buick in '98. Have a good morning, dear.

Mavis gives a slow wave, turns, and goes back inside. The porch light clicks off. Daniel stares at her dark house, his breathing ragged.

**What Changes:** Daniel realizes his operational security is already compromised before the sun has even come up.

## SCENE 2: THE KITCHEN (6:30 AM)
**Scene Description:** The Cross kitchen. NINA CROSS is packing lunches. She is precise, efficient. Daniel enters from the garage. He smells strongly of bleach and sweat. He goes to the sink and washes his hands for far too long.
**Emotional Objective:** Establish the dynamic between Daniel and Nina. She is the observer; he is the reactor.
**Subtext:** Nina knows he has done something terrible. She is actively choosing not to ask what it is, because asking makes her an accessory.
**Performance Tone:** Quiet, strained domesticity. The tension is in what isn't being said.

**NINA**
(Not looking up from the sandwiches)
You used the industrial bleach. Under the sink.

**DANIEL**
(Drying his hands, trying to sound casual)
Yeah. Had a spill in the trunk. That liquid fertilizer from the garden center. Smelled awful.

Nina stops spreading mayonnaise. She looks at the bottle of bleach on the counter, then at Daniel. She doesn't say anything for three full seconds. The silence is crushing.

**NINA**
The fertilizer is granular, Daniel. It’s in bags. In the shed.

Daniel stops drying his hands. He looks at her. He doesn't have an excuse ready.

**DANIEL**
Nina...

**NINA**
(Cutting him off, voice completely flat)
Don't.

She walks over to him, hands him a mug of coffee, and looks down at his feet.

**NINA**
You missed a spot on your left shoe. Go change. Mia’s going to be down in five minutes and I don't want her asking why the house smells like a hospital.

**What Changes:** The audience learns that Nina is not a victim or a fool. She is a pragmatic protector who will fortify the house without needing a confession.

## SCENE 3: THE WELCOME AT THE DOOR (10:00 AM)
**Scene Description:** The Vale family (MARCUS, CELESTE, ADRIAN, JADEN) stand on the Cross family's porch. Celeste holds an impossibly perfect basket of baked goods. Nina opens the door. The visual contrast is immediate: the Vales look like a magazine cover; Nina looks like a woman who hasn't slept.
**Emotional Objective:** Establish the class tension and the Vales' aggressive, performative perfection.
**Subtext:** Celeste is establishing dominance. Marcus is assessing weaknesses. Nina is trying to hold the line.
**Performance Tone:** Weaponized politeness. Every compliment is a tiny knife.

**CELESTE**
(Radiant, stepping forward before Nina can fully open the door)
Nina! I hope we aren't interrupting. We just realized we’ve been across the street for a week and haven’t officially introduced ourselves. I’m Celeste. This is Marcus.

**NINA**
(Forcing a smile, blocking the doorway slightly)
Hi. No, it’s fine. Thank you. We’ve just been... settling in.

**MARCUS**
(Calm, sparse, looking past Nina into the house)
It’s a beautiful property. Good bones. I noticed you're replacing the security system.

Nina stiffens. Daniel appears behind her, trying to project warmth.

**DANIEL**
Just an upgrade. The old one was glitchy. I’m Daniel. Good to meet you.

**CELESTE**
(Handing the basket to Nina)
Sourdough and rosemary. I bake when I’m stressed about moving. And these are the boys. Adrian, Jaden.

Adrian offers a charming, easy smile. Jaden barely looks up from his phone, offering a half-nod.

**ADRIAN**
(Charming, verbally agile)
She bakes when she’s stressed, which means we’ve been eating nothing but carbohydrates for six days. Nice to meet you, Mr. Cross.

**MARCUS**
(Looking directly at Daniel)
We should get a drink. Tonight. Come over. We’ve got the patio set up. Nothing formal.

**DANIEL**
(Looking at Nina, looking for an out)
Oh, we’d love to, but we’re still unpacking boxes in the living room—

**CELESTE**
(Soft, cutting)
Nonsense. The boxes will be there tomorrow. Seven o'clock? We insist.

**NINA**
(Realizing they cannot say no without looking suspicious)
Seven is perfect. Thank you.

**What Changes:** The Vales have successfully invaded the Cross family's perimeter. The dinner invitation is not a request; it is a summons.

## SCENE 4: MIA AND JADEN (4:00 PM)
**Scene Description:** The street curb. MIA CROSS is struggling to drag two exceptionally heavy garbage cans to the curb. JADEN VALE is sitting on the hood of his brother’s parked car across the street, watching her struggle. He doesn't offer to help.
**Emotional Objective:** Establish Mia's directness and Jaden's guarded, observant nature.
**Subtext:** Mia is frustrated by the heavy bags (which Daniel packed the night before). Jaden is assessing her as a variable.
**Performance Tone:** Teen awkwardness masking genuine intelligence. Flirtatious hostility.

**MIA**
(Dropping a bag, panting, glaring across the street)
You're not going to help. You're just going to watch.

**JADEN**
(Without looking up from his phone)
I’m on break.

**MIA**
From what? Sitting?

Jaden finally looks up. He takes in the sweat on her forehead, the sheer weight of the bag she’s dragging. He slides off the hood and walks over slowly.

**JADEN**
(Guarded, cool)
You guys throw away a lot of rocks or something? That thing looks like it weighs ninety pounds.

**MIA**
(Kicking the bag, frustrated)
I don't know. My dad packed them last night. He's on a cleaning tear.

Jaden reaches down, grabs the handle of the bag, and lifts it effortlessly into the bin. He doesn't brag about it. He just does it.

**JADEN**
(Understated)
He must have been cleaning the whole garage.

**MIA**
(Looking at him, a little defensive but curious)
Thanks. You're Jaden, right? The quiet one. Your mom came over this morning. She brought bread.

**JADEN**
(A small, dry smile)
Yeah. Sourdough. It’s a tactical strike. She uses carbs to soften up the neighbors.

**MIA**
(Laughs, surprised by his honesty)
Well, it worked. My dad ate half a loaf.

**JADEN**
(Looking at the Cross house, his eyes narrowing slightly)
Just tell him not to eat the crust. That’s where she hides the poison.

**What Changes:** Mia realizes Jaden is not a generic suburban teen; he is highly observant and quietly rebellious against his own family. Jaden realizes Mia is honest, but her father is hiding something heavy in the trash.

## SCENE 5: MAVIS INTRUDES (6:45 PM)
**Scene Description:** The Cross living room. Daniel and Nina are getting ready for the dinner at the Vales. Daniel is frantic, looking for his watch. The doorbell rings. Daniel freezes. Nina opens the door. It’s MAVIS WHITLOCK, holding a small Tupperware container.
**Emotional Objective:** Mavis weaponizes her age to invade their space and gather intel.
**Subtext:** Mavis is probing for weaknesses. Nina is trying to block her without being rude. Daniel is terrified she knows about the trunk.
**Performance Tone:** Socially invasive politeness. A comedic pressure cooker.

**MAVIS**
(Stepping inside before Nina can stop her)
Nina, dear. I brought you some leftover casserole. I know moving is a nightmare. I saw Daniel up at four this morning scrubbing his car, so I thought, 'Those poor people haven't had time to cook.'

Daniel appears in the hallway. He looks like he’s about to have a heart attack.

**DANIEL**
(Voice strained)
Mavis. Thank you. That’s very... observant of you.

**MAVIS**
(Smiling, handing the container to Nina)
Oh, I don't sleep much. At my age, sleep is just a rehearsal. You know, Daniel, I noticed you started backing into the driveway today.

**DANIEL**
(Swallowing hard)
Just... easier to pull out in the morning.

**MAVIS**
(Nodding slowly, eyes tracking every detail of his face)
Of course it is. Quicker getaway. My late husband used to back in when he owed money to the bookies in '82. He said it saved him three seconds. He was wrong, of course. They broke his legs anyway.

Nina steps smoothly between Mavis and Daniel, physically breaking the line of sight.

**NINA**
(Controlled, polite but firm)
Thank you for the casserole, Mavis. We’re actually just heading across the street for dinner. The Vales invited us.

**MAVIS**
(Her smile falters slightly; she adjusts her glasses)
The Vales. Oh. Well. Be careful over there, dear. Celeste’s wine is excellent, but her questions are very expensive.

**What Changes:** Mavis establishes herself as a constant, unpredictable threat. She has noticed the logistical failures (the car washing, the backing in) and forced Daniel to realize he is being watched constantly.

## SCENE 6: THE VALE DINNER (8:00 PM)
**Scene Description:** The Vale patio. Perfect lighting. Expensive wine. Marcus is pouring drinks. Celeste is directing the conversation. Daniel is trying to act normal. Nina is watching Celeste.
**Emotional Objective:** The Vales are testing the Cross family. The Crosses are trying to survive the interrogation.
**Subtext:** Every polite question is a probe for weakness.
**Performance Tone:** High-class tension. Criminal-coded politeness masking absolute ruthlessness.

**MARCUS**
(Pouring wine, precise and calm)
So, Daniel. Logistics. That’s a tough game right now. Supply chains are a mess. How are you holding up?

**DANIEL**
(Taking a sip, trying to sound relaxed)
It’s fine. We manage. You just have to... adapt. Find new routes.

**CELESTE**
(Soft-spoken, cutting)
Adaptability is so important. Marcus is in acquisitions. He says the hardest part isn't finding the asset, it’s making sure the current owner realizes they don't want it anymore.

Nina looks sharply at Celeste.

**NINA**
That sounds less like acquisitions and more like hostile takeovers.

**MARCUS**
(A dry, quiet laugh)
The line is very thin, Nina. It usually depends on who has the better lawyers.

**CELESTE**
(Turning to Daniel, her voice dripping with sympathy)
You look exhausted, Daniel. Truly. Are you sleeping at all? Moving is stressful, but you look like a man carrying the weight of the world.

**DANIEL**
(Gripping his wine glass too tightly)
I’m fine. Just... adjusting to the new neighborhood.

**MARCUS**
(Leaning forward slightly, the intimidation quiet but absolute)
It’s a very safe street, Daniel. We make sure of it. Nothing happens here without us knowing. If you ever have a problem... an unexpected debt, a logistical failure... you come to me. We protect our own.

**What Changes:** The dinner shifts from a neighborly welcome to a quiet, terrifying assertion of dominance. The Vales are not just wealthy neighbors; they are predators who smell blood in the water.

## SCENE 7: NINA AND CELESTE PRIVATE SCENE (8:45 PM)
**Scene Description:** The Vale kitchen. Celeste is pouring a second bottle of wine. Nina has followed her in to help, but really to assess her away from Marcus.
**Emotional Objective:** Celeste tries to charm and dominate Nina. Nina refuses to be charmed, establishing herself as Celeste’s intellectual equal.
**Subtext:** Celeste is looking for the cracks in Nina’s marriage. Nina is showing Celeste that she sees exactly what she is doing.
**Performance Tone:** Velvet hostility. Two highly intelligent women circling each other.

**CELESTE**
(Pouring the wine, her voice low and intimate)
You know, when we first moved here, I hated it. The silence. It feels like everyone is just waiting for someone else to make a mistake so they have something to talk about.

**NINA**
(Leaning against the counter, composed)
I imagine that’s true of most places with this kind of property tax.

**CELESTE**
(Laughs softly, handing Nina a glass)
Exactly. But Daniel... he seems like a good man. Very earnest. He must be under a lot of pressure right now. The logistics business is brutal.

**NINA**
(Taking the glass, not drinking)
He handles pressure well. He’s very organized.

**CELESTE**
(Taking a slow sip, her eyes never leaving Nina’s)
Is he? That’s good. Marcus always says the organized ones break the hardest when the system fails. Because they don't know how to improvise. 

**NINA**
(A tight, cold smile)
That’s a very cynical way to look at the world, Celeste.

**CELESTE**
(Softly, stepping slightly closer)
It’s a very safe way to look at the world, Nina. I just want you to know... if things get complicated, if Daniel’s organization starts to fail... you can come to me. We take care of our friends.

**NINA**
(Setting the wine glass down, her voice dropping a register)
I appreciate the offer. But my husband and I don't require taking care of. We handle our own messes.

**What Changes:** Celeste realizes Nina is not a weak, panicked suburban wife. Nina realizes Celeste is actively probing their marriage for a vulnerability to exploit. The battle lines are drawn.

## SCENE 8: THE SMALL FAVOR (9:15 PM)
**Scene Description:** The Vale driveway. Daniel and Nina are leaving. Marcus walks them out. He stops Daniel near his car.
**Emotional Objective:** Marcus asserts dominance by making a request Daniel cannot refuse without looking suspicious.
**Subtext:** Marcus knows Daniel is hiding something. He is testing how far Daniel will bend to avoid drawing attention.
**Performance Tone:** Quietly intimidating, dryly funny, but absolutely unyielding.

**MARCUS**
(Hands in his pockets, perfectly relaxed)
Daniel. Before you go. I need a small favor.

**DANIEL**
(Already defensive, trying to sound casual)
Sure. What is it?

**MARCUS**
(Looking at Daniel’s SUV)
My brother-in-law is flying in tomorrow night. Needs a pickup from the private airfield out by Route 9. My driver is out sick, and my car is in the shop. You’ve got the space. 

**DANIEL**
(Panic flaring in his eyes)
Tomorrow night? I’m... I have a lot of unpacking to do. And it’s a long drive.

**MARCUS**
(A dry, sparse smile)
It’s an hour each way. He’s got some heavy bags. You clearly know how to pack a trunk, Daniel. I saw you out here at four in the morning making sure it was spotless.

Daniel freezes. The blood drains from his face. Marcus’s voice remains entirely conversational.

**MARCUS**
(Stepping closer, his voice dropping)
It’s just a ride, Daniel. A neighborly favor. You’ll pick him up at midnight. He’ll give you the address. 

**DANIEL**
(Trapped, swallowing hard)
Midnight. Okay. Yeah, I can do that.

**MARCUS**
(Patting Daniel on the shoulder, a little too hard)
I appreciate it. Sleep well, Daniel. You look like you need it.

**What Changes:** Daniel is officially compromised. He is now performing a task for a criminal organization simply to prevent them from looking closer at his own crime.

## SCENE 9: THE DETECTIVES ARRIVE (11:00 PM)
**Scene Description:** The Cross living room. Daniel is pacing. Nina is sitting on the couch, watching him. The doorbell rings. It’s late. Too late for neighbors. Daniel opens the door. DETECTIVE NATHAN COLE and DETECTIVE LENA MERCER are standing on the porch.
**Emotional Objective:** The sudden, terrifying intrusion of the law. The detectives are not aggressive; they are deceptively calm.
**Subtext:** Cole and Mercer already suspect Daniel. They are just watching him react.
**Performance Tone:** Dry, measured, probing. The pressure of the state colliding with suburban panic.

**COLE**
(Holding up a badge, his voice entirely flat)
Mr. Cross? Detective Cole. This is Detective Mercer. Apologies for the hour.

**DANIEL**
(Voice shaking slightly)
Is something wrong? Did something happen in the neighborhood?

**MERCER**
(Precise, quietly probing)
Not in the neighborhood, no. We’re actually investigating an incident at a warehouse facility on the south side. Your company’s name came up on a manifest.

**NINA**
(Stepping up beside Daniel, her voice perfectly controlled)
My husband’s company handles logistics for hundreds of warehouses, Detectives. Is there a specific shipment you’re looking for?

**COLE**
(Looking at Nina, assessing her instantly)
A specific vehicle, actually. A dark SUV. Like the one in your driveway. 

**DANIEL**
(Over-explaining, panicked)
That’s a very common car. Half the street drives one.

**MERCER**
(A small, empathetic smile that is entirely fake)
It is common. But not many of them smell like industrial bleach from the sidewalk, Mr. Cross.

Cole doesn't smile. He just stares at Daniel.

**COLE**
(Dry, deceptively simple)
We’re just doing the rounds tonight. Eliminating variables. We’ll likely need to speak with you tomorrow when you’re less... tired. Have a good night, folks.

The detectives turn and walk away. Daniel closes the door, leaning his forehead against the wood, completely broken. Nina stares at his back.

**What Changes:** The pilot ends. The audience knows the Vales are manipulating Daniel, Mavis is watching him, and the police are already at his door. There is no escape. The trap is closed.
