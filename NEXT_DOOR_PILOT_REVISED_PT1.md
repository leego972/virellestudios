# NEXT DOOR: PILOT SCRIPT (REVISED - PART 1)

## SCENE 1: THE TRUNK (4:30 AM)
**MAVIS**
(Calling out, voice carrying too far in the quiet)
Bit early for spring cleaning, isn't it, Daniel? Unless the pollen count is getting aggressive.

Daniel freezes. He slowly turns around. He tries to arrange his face into a normal, neighborly expression. It looks like a hostage video.

**DANIEL**
(Voice tight, slightly too loud)
Mavis. Hi. Yeah, just... spilled some fertilizer. Didn't want it to set into the carpet.

**MAVIS**
(Takes a slow sip from her mug)
Fertilizer. At four in the morning. You must really love that lawn.

**DANIEL**
Couldn't sleep.

**MAVIS**
(Smiling, entirely unbothered by his panic)
Well, you make sure you get the edges. That stuff eats right through the lining. Ruined my Buick in '98. Though I suppose my husband’s cigar ash didn't help. Have a good morning, dear. Try not to scrub the paint off.

---

## SCENE 2: THE KITCHEN (6:30 AM)
**NINA**
(Not looking up from the sandwiches)
You used the industrial bleach. Under the sink.

**DANIEL**
(Drying his hands, trying to sound casual)
Yeah. Had a spill in the trunk. That liquid fertilizer from the garden center. Smelled awful.

Nina stops spreading mayonnaise. She looks at the bottle of bleach on the counter, then at Daniel.

**NINA**
The fertilizer is granular, Daniel. It’s in bags. In the shed. And it doesn't smell like a crime scene in a hospital.

Daniel stops drying his hands. He looks at her. He doesn't have an excuse ready.

**DANIEL**
Nina...

**NINA**
(Cutting him off, voice completely flat)
Don't. I haven't had enough coffee to pretend I believe you.

She walks over to him, hands him a mug of coffee, and looks down at his feet.

**NINA**
You missed a spot on your left shoe. Go change. Mia’s going to be down in five minutes and I don't want her asking why you smell like a swimming pool.

---

## SCENE 3: THE WELCOME AT THE DOOR (10:00 AM)
**CELESTE**
(Radiant, stepping forward before Nina can fully open the door)
Nina! I hope we aren't interrupting. We just realized we’ve been across the street for a week and haven’t officially introduced ourselves. I’m Celeste. This is Marcus.

**NINA**
(Forcing a smile, blocking the doorway slightly)
Hi. No, it’s fine. Thank you. We’ve just been... settling in.

**MARCUS**
(Calm, sparse, looking past Nina into the house)
It’s a beautiful property. Good bones. I noticed you're replacing the security system. The old one was a little... optimistic.

Nina stiffens. Daniel appears behind her, trying to project warmth.

**DANIEL**
Just an upgrade. The old one was glitchy. Kept going off when the wind blew. I’m Daniel. Good to meet you.

**CELESTE**
(Handing the basket to Nina)
Sourdough and rosemary. I bake when I’m stressed about moving. And these are the boys. Adrian, Jaden.

Adrian offers a charming, easy smile. Jaden barely looks up from his phone.

**ADRIAN**
(Charming, verbally agile)
She bakes when she’s stressed, which means we’ve been eating nothing but carbohydrates for six days. If she gets any more anxious, we’ll be opening a bakery. Nice to meet you, Mr. Cross.

**MARCUS**
(Looking directly at Daniel)
We should get a drink. Tonight. Come over. We’ve got the patio set up. Nothing formal.

**DANIEL**
(Looking at Nina, looking for an out)
Oh, we’d love to, but we’re still unpacking boxes in the living room—

**CELESTE**
(Soft, cutting)
Nonsense. The boxes will be there tomorrow. They don't run away. Seven o'clock? We insist.

**NINA**
(Realizing they cannot say no without looking suspicious)
Seven is perfect. Thank you. We'll bring the wine.

**CELESTE**
(Smiling)
Oh, don't worry about that. We have a cellar. Just bring yourselves.

---

## SCENE 4: MIA AND JADEN (4:00 PM)
**MIA**
(Dropping a bag, panting, glaring across the street)
You're not going to help. You're just going to watch.

**JADEN**
(Without looking up from his phone)
I’m on break.

**MIA**
From what? Sitting?

Jaden finally looks up. He slides off the hood and walks over slowly.

**JADEN**
(Guarded, cool)
You guys throw away a lot of rocks or something? That thing looks like it weighs ninety pounds.

**MIA**
(Kicking the bag, frustrated)
I don't know. My dad packed them last night. He's on a cleaning tear.

Jaden reaches down, grabs the handle of the bag, and lifts it effortlessly into the bin.

**JADEN**
(Understated)
He must have been cleaning the whole garage. And maybe the foundation.

**MIA**
(Looking at him, a little defensive but curious)
Thanks. You're Jaden, right? The quiet one. Your mom came over this morning. She brought bread.

**JADEN**
(A small, dry smile)
Yeah. Sourdough. It’s a tactical strike. She uses carbs to soften up the neighbors.

**MIA**
(Laughs, surprised by his honesty)
Well, it worked. My dad ate half a loaf.

**JADEN**
(Looking at the Cross house, his eyes narrowing slightly)
Just tell him not to eat the crust. That’s where she hides the poison. And maybe tell him to lift with his legs next time.

---

## SCENE 5: MAVIS INTRUDES (6:45 PM)
**MAVIS**
(Stepping inside before Nina can stop her)
Nina, dear. I brought you some leftover casserole. I know moving is a nightmare. I saw Daniel up at four this morning scrubbing his car, so I thought, 'Those poor people haven't had time to cook.'

Daniel appears in the hallway. He looks like he’s about to have a heart attack.

**DANIEL**
(Voice strained)
Mavis. Thank you. That’s very... observant of you.

**MAVIS**
(Smiling, handing the container to Nina)
Oh, I don't sleep much. At my age, sleep is just a rehearsal. You know, Daniel, I noticed you started backing into the driveway today.

**DANIEL**
(Swallowing hard)
Just... easier to pull out in the morning.

**MAVIS**
(Nodding slowly, eyes tracking every detail of his face)
Of course it is. Quicker getaway. My late husband used to back in when he owed money to the bookies in '82. He said it saved him three seconds. He was wrong, of course. They broke his legs anyway. But he did look very efficient right up until the end.

Nina steps smoothly between Mavis and Daniel, physically breaking the line of sight.

**NINA**
(Controlled, polite but firm)
Thank you for the casserole, Mavis. We’re actually just heading across the street for dinner. The Vales invited us.

**MAVIS**
(Her smile falters slightly; she adjusts her glasses)
The Vales. Oh. Well. Be careful over there, dear. Celeste’s wine is excellent, but her questions are very expensive. Don't let her fill your glass too high.
