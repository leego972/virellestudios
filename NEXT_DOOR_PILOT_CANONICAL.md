# NEXT DOOR: PILOT SCRIPT (CANONICAL)

## SCENE 1: THE TRUNK (4:30 AM)
**EXT. CROSS DRIVEWAY – PRE-DAWN**
Daniel is scrubbing the trunk alone in the dark. The smell of industrial bleach is sharp in the cold air.

**MAVIS**
(Calling out, voice carrying too far in the quiet)
Bit early for spring cleaning, isn't it, Daniel? Unless the pollen count is getting aggressive.

Daniel freezes. He slowly turns around. He tries to arrange his face into a normal, neighborly expression. It looks like a hostage video.

**DANIEL**
(Voice tight, slightly too loud)
Mavis. Hi. Yeah, just... spilled some fertilizer. Didn't want it to set into the carpet.

**MAVIS**
(Takes a slow sip from her mug)
Fertilizer. At four in the morning. You must really love that lawn.

**DANIEL**
Couldn't sleep.

**MAVIS**
(Smiling, entirely unbothered by his panic)
Well, you make sure you get the edges. That stuff eats right through the lining. Ruined my Buick in '98. Though I suppose my husband’s cigar ash didn't help. Have a good morning, dear. Try not to scrub the paint off.

## SCENE 2: THE KITCHEN (6:30 AM)
**INT. CROSS KITCHEN – MORNING**
**NINA**
(Not looking up from the sandwiches)
You used the industrial bleach. Under the sink.

**DANIEL**
(Drying his hands, trying to sound casual)
Yeah. Had a spill in the trunk. That liquid fertilizer from the garden center. Smelled awful.

Nina stops spreading mayonnaise. She looks at the bottle of bleach on the counter, then at Daniel.

**NINA**
The fertilizer is granular, Daniel. It’s in bags. In the shed. And it doesn't smell like a crime scene in a hospital.

Daniel stops drying his hands. He looks at her. He doesn't have an excuse ready.

**DANIEL**
Nina...

**NINA**
(Cutting him off, voice completely flat)
Don't. I haven't had enough coffee to pretend I believe you.

She walks over to him, hands him a mug of coffee, and looks down at his feet.

**NINA**
You missed a spot on your left shoe. Go change. Mia’s going to be down in five minutes and I don't want her asking why you smell like a swimming pool.

---

## SCENE 3: THE WELCOME AT THE DOOR (10:00 AM)
**EXT. CROSS HOUSE – FRONT PORCH – LATE MORNING**
Bright late-morning light. Expensive quiet. The kind of suburban stillness that makes every sound feel observed.

NINA opens the front door halfway.

On the porch: CELESTE, immaculate, holding a beautifully assembled welcome basket. MARCUS stands half a step behind her, hands in coat pockets. ADRIAN leans on the porch railing like he already owns the place. JADEN lingers farther back, phone in hand, not pretending to care.

**CELESTE**
Nina. I hope we’re not interrupting. We realized we’d committed the cardinal sin of civilized living.

**NINA**
Which is?

**CELESTE**
Being seen before being introduced.

A beat. Nina gives a polite smile that does not invite entry.

**NINA**
That does sound serious.

**CELESTE**
It is on a street like this. People get very creative when they have no facts.

She lifts the basket slightly.

**CELESTE (CONT’D)**
Sourdough. Rosemary crackers. Preserves I didn’t make but will lie about if necessary.

DANIEL appears behind Nina, wiping his hands on a dish towel, trying to seem casual and absolutely failing.

**DANIEL**
That’s... wow. That’s very kind.

**ADRIAN**
She stress-bakes like other people issue warnings.

**CELESTE**
Adrian.

**ADRIAN**
What? It’s neighborly to be transparent.

Marcus finally steps just enough into the light to properly register.

**MARCUS**
Marcus Vale.

He says it simply. No smile. Daniel steps forward instinctively and shakes his hand.

**DANIEL**
Daniel Cross. Good to meet you.

Marcus studies him a fraction too long.

**MARCUS**
Likewise.

His gaze drifts briefly past Nina into the house. Boxes. Half-opened drawers. Disorder.

**MARCUS (CONT’D)**
Moving is exhausting. You can always tell how tired a family is by how quickly the hallway fills with things they meant to put away properly.

Nina catches the line for what it is: an observation disguised as sympathy.

**NINA**
Then I suppose we’re transparent already.

**CELESTE**
Only to people looking too hard.

A small beat. Celeste smiles.

**CELESTE (CONT’D)**
Which, sadly, is most of the street.

Adrian glances toward the curb.

**ADRIAN**
Mavis across the road has probably already assigned everyone a backstory.

**JADEN**
Without dialogue. She likes to work visually.

That gets the smallest surprised laugh out of Nina.

**DANIEL**
You know Mavis well?

**CELESTE**
Everyone knows Mavis. Survival here requires local literacy.

She hands the basket to Nina.

**CELESTE (CONT’D)**
We’re having drinks this evening. Patio. Nothing formal.

**NINA**
That’s generous, but we’re still unpacking—

**CELESTE**
So unpack tomorrow.

Softly said. Not quite a command. Not quite not.

**ADRIAN**
Please come. If it’s awful, you’ll at least leave with better wine than you currently own.

**DANIEL**
We don’t—

He stops himself. Too fast. Adrian smiles like he’s found the exact place to stand to make Daniel uncomfortable.

**ADRIAN**
Relax. Everybody on this street is pretending to be less curious than they are.

Marcus looks directly at Daniel.

**MARCUS**
Seven.

Not a question. Daniel glances at Nina. Nina sees there is no elegant refusal left.

**NINA**
Seven is fine.

**CELESTE**
Perfect.

A beat.

**CELESTE (CONT’D)**
And Nina?

**NINA**
Yes?

**CELESTE**
Do not bother tidying for us. Disorder is honest. It’s usually the nicest thing in a house.

Celeste turns. Marcus follows. Jaden pushes off the rail. Adrian lingers one second longer.

**ADRIAN**
Welcome to the neighborhood.

Then, lower—

**ADRIAN (CONT’D)**
Try not to let anyone make you feel too ordinary too quickly.

He smiles and goes. Nina closes the door. Silence.

**DANIEL**
Well.

**NINA**
No.

**DANIEL**
I just said “well.”

**NINA**
And I’m answering in advance.

## SCENE 4: MIA AND JADEN (4:00 PM)
**EXT. STREET CURB / DRIVEWAY LINE – LATE AFTERNOON**
Soft late-afternoon light. Heat easing. Bin day. MIA drags an overstuffed garbage bag toward the curb, muttering under her breath.

Across the street, JADEN sits on the hood of a dark car, watching without helping.

**MIA**
You planning to help or are you doing some kind of rich-person field study?

**JADEN**
I’m observing local customs.

**MIA**
And what have you learned?

**JADEN**
Your family throws away things like you’re fleeing a country.

Mia drops the bag with a thud.

**MIA**
Great. I always wanted to be judged by a boy sitting on a car he definitely didn’t buy.

Jaden finally looks up properly. A small smile.

**JADEN**
Technically, none of us buy anything. My father acquires.

**MIA**
That sounds illegal.

**JADEN**
It sounds expensive. Different department.

He hops down, crosses over, grabs the bag, and lifts it into the bin.

**MIA**
Thanks.

**JADEN**
Don’t get sentimental.

**MIA**
Too late. I’m writing about this in my diary.

**JADEN**
You seem like a notes app person.

**MIA**
You seem like someone who says two words all day and expects people to call it mystery.

That lands. Jaden almost smiles.

**JADEN**
Three words, actually. I pace myself.

**MIA**
So what’s wrong with your family?

**JADEN**
You start easy, huh?

**MIA**
I just met your uncle. That felt medically significant.

**JADEN**
That’s Adrian. He thinks being charming is a constitutional right.

**MIA**
He talks like he’s auditioning to ruin somebody’s life.

**JADEN**
That is, unfortunately, one of his better skills.

Mia studies him.

**MIA**
And your parents?

**JADEN**
My mother collects information and serves fish.
My father doesn’t repeat himself.
It’s a very efficient household.

**MIA**
You say that like you’re joking.

**JADEN**
I say most things like that.

He glances at the heavy bags still near the garage.

**JADEN (CONT’D)**
Your dad always throw out half the house at sunset?

**MIA**
No. He’s been weird since last night.

**JADEN**
Weird how?

**MIA**
Like he’s trying to act normal in front of people who already know he isn’t.

A beat. Jaden clocking that.

**JADEN**
That’s not weird. That’s adulthood.

Mia laughs despite herself.

**MIA**
You are so weirdly eighty years old.

**JADEN**
Comes from the neighborhood.

---

## SCENE 5: MAVIS INTRUDES (6:45 PM)
**INT. CROSS HOUSE – LIVING ROOM / ENTRY – EARLY EVENING**
The Cross house is half-unpacked. Daniel is looking for his watch. Nina is putting on earrings in the reflection of a dark TV screen.

Doorbell. Daniel freezes.

**NINA**
If that’s them, and you open the door looking guilty, I will actually divorce you.

**DANIEL**
I don’t look guilty.

**NINA**
You look like you buried a senator.

She opens the door. MAVIS stands there with a casserole dish and the expression of a woman bringing aid to a disaster zone she hopes gets worse.

**MAVIS**
Hello, darlings. I brought food and unsolicited concern.

**NINA**
Mavis.

**MAVIS**
I know. Lucky you.

She steps in before being invited.

**MAVIS (CONT’D)**
I saw Daniel up at four this morning scrubbing the trunk of the car, so I thought, those people are either underfed or in trouble, and either way a casserole helps.

Daniel goes still.

**DANIEL**
You really do see everything, huh?

**MAVIS**
No, dear. I just notice what people wish counted as nothing.

She hands the casserole to Nina.

**MAVIS (CONT’D)**
Chicken. Or an approximation. The butcher’s grandson drinks.

**NINA**
That’s... reassuring.

**MAVIS**
In this neighborhood, reassurance is usually a sales tactic.

She turns to Daniel.

**MAVIS (CONT’D)**
You started backing into the driveway today.

**DANIEL**
It’s easier in the morning.

**MAVIS**
Mm. Men only change parking habits for three reasons: fear, infidelity, or an inflated opinion of their own schedule.

Nina takes one subtle step between them.

**NINA**
We’re actually just heading out.

**MAVIS**
Across the street?

**NINA**
Yes.

MAVIS smiles without warmth.

**MAVIS**
Oh, lovely. Celeste is a marvelous hostess. She pours wine like she’s granting absolution.

**DANIEL**
They seem nice.

**MAVIS**
So did my second dentist. He stole from widows.

A beat.

**MAVIS (CONT’D)**
Be careful over there, Nina.

**NINA**
Of what?

**MAVIS**
Of enjoying yourself. It blurs judgement faster than alcohol.

She turns to go, then pauses at the door.

**MAVIS (CONT’D)**
And Daniel?

**DANIEL**
Yeah?

**MAVIS**
If you’re going to panic-clean evidence before sunrise, use less bleach. It travels.

She exits. Silence. Daniel stares at the door.

**DANIEL**
I hate her.

**NINA**
No, you fear her.
Different category.

---

## SCENE 6: THE VALE DINNER (8:00 PM)
**EXT. VALE PATIO – NIGHT**
The patio is immaculate. Architectural lighting. Beautiful glasses. Too much calm.

Marcus pours wine with unnerving precision. Celeste sits like she designed the furniture herself. Adrian lounges. Jaden eats quietly, watching everyone. Daniel and Nina sit opposite, trying not to look like they’ve been invited into something ceremonial.

**ADRIAN**
This is the part where we pretend this is casual.

**DANIEL**
Is it not?

**ADRIAN**
No. Casual people use paper napkins.

Celeste gives Adrian a look. He lifts his glass in apology.

**CELESTE**
Ignore him. He mistakes performance for honesty.

**ADRIAN**
And you mistake control for grace. We all have hobbies.

Marcus hands Daniel a glass.

**MARCUS**
What exactly do you handle at the depot?

**DANIEL**
Route allocation, timing, overflow, late manifests. Mostly boring things.

**MARCUS**
Nothing is boring if enough people rely on it.

**NINA**
That sounds suspiciously like respect for infrastructure.

Marcus glances at her.

**MARCUS**
I respect anything people take for granted until it fails.

Celeste turns to Nina.

**CELESTE**
Marcus admires systems. It’s one of the few things that makes him sentimental.

**ADRIAN**
That and imported flooring.

**JADEN**
And silence.

Marcus does not react.

**DANIEL**
So what do you do exactly?

A tiny pause.

**CELESTE**
Acquisitions.

**NINA**
That can mean almost anything.

**CELESTE**
Exactly. It’s why people use it.

**ADRIAN**
It means if someone has something my brother wants, eventually they develop a healthier perspective on ownership.

Daniel laughs, then realizes maybe he shouldn’t have.

**MARCUS**
Adrian enjoys drama.

**ADRIAN**
I enjoy accuracy with better posture.

Celeste watches Daniel closely.

**CELESTE**
You look exhausted.

**DANIEL**
Long week.

**CELESTE**
No. Exhausted is different. Long weeks sit in the shoulders. This is lower. This is moral.

Daniel grips the stem of his glass too hard.

**NINA**
That’s an ambitious diagnosis over first wine.

**CELESTE**
I don’t diagnose. I notice.

Jaden, quietly to Mia—

**JADEN**
That’s our version of affection.

Mia hides a smile. Marcus leans slightly toward Daniel.

**MARCUS**
If you ever run into a problem on this street, you come to me.

**DANIEL**
What kind of problem?

**MARCUS**
The sort that gets worse when handled publicly.

A beat.

**ADRIAN**
He means he’s neighborly in a very specific jurisdiction.

**NINA**
And what jurisdiction is that?

Marcus finally smiles, barely.

**MARCUS**
The practical one.

A tense beat. Celeste lifts her glass.

**CELESTE**
To good neighbors.

Nina lifts hers too.

**NINA**
Temporary category.

Celeste smiles.

**CELESTE**
All categories are temporary. That’s what makes them useful.

---

## SCENE 7: NINA AND CELESTE PRIVATE SCENE (8:45 PM)
**INT. VALE KITCHEN – NIGHT**
The kitchen is expensive and spotless. Too spotless. Celeste opens a second bottle of wine. Nina follows with her empty glass, but both know this is not about wine.

**CELESTE**
I hated this street when we first arrived.

**NINA**
You hide it well.

**CELESTE**
I hide most things well.

She pours.

**CELESTE (CONT’D)**
It’s the silence here. In cities, people stare at you honestly. In places like this, they do it from behind good curtains and charity committees.

**NINA**
That’s what property taxes buy. Landscaping and passive aggression.

Celeste laughs softly.

**CELESTE**
You’re sharper than Daniel.

**NINA**
He’d be relieved to hear that. He’s carrying enough.

Celeste hands her the glass, watching.

**CELESTE**
Yes. He is.

**NINA**
You say that like you know him.

**CELESTE**
I know the look.
Good men under pressure always behave like the universe has become a paperwork issue.

Nina does not drink.

**NINA**
And what do bad men do?

**CELESTE**
Hire better help.

A beat.

**NINA**
You’ve decided we need help very quickly.

**CELESTE**
No. I’ve decided you’re the sort of woman who would rather drown quietly than ask for a towel.

That lands.

**NINA**
And you’re the sort of woman who offers one after checking what’s in my pockets.

Celeste smiles. No offense taken.

**CELESTE**
Now we’re speaking honestly.

She steps a little closer, not intimate exactly, but unmistakably inside normal social distance.

**CELESTE (CONT’D)**
If things become complicated, Nina, come to me.
Do not let Daniel improvise. Men like him mistake motion for control.

**NINA**
And women like you mistake intervention for kindness.

**CELESTE**
Kindness is decorative. I prefer usefulness.

Nina sets the glass down untouched.

**NINA**
My family does not need rescuing.

**CELESTE**
Everyone says that right before they begin negotiating.

---

## SCENE 8: THE SMALL FAVOR (9:15 PM)
**EXT. VALE DRIVEWAY – NIGHT**
The Crosses are leaving. Cool night air. Distant insects. The house behind Marcus glows like a controlled fire.

Marcus walks Daniel to the car. Just Daniel.

**MARCUS**
Before you go, I need a small favor.

**DANIEL**
What kind of favor?

**MARCUS**
The useful kind.

A beat.

**MARCUS (CONT’D)**
My driver’s out. Someone is coming in late tomorrow from a private airfield off Route Nine. I need him collected.

**DANIEL**
Tomorrow night?

**MARCUS**
Midnight.

**DANIEL**
That’s... late.

**MARCUS**
Planes are inconsiderate.

Daniel forces a laugh. It dies immediately.

**DANIEL**
I don’t know if I can—

**MARCUS**
You can.

Not louder. Just simpler. Marcus glances at Daniel’s SUV.

**MARCUS (CONT’D)**
Plenty of room. Clean trunk.

Daniel freezes.

**MARCUS (CONT’D)**
I noticed your work this morning.
Very thorough.

**DANIEL**
You were awake?

**MARCUS**
No. Mavis was.

A beat. Marcus lets that sit.

**MARCUS (CONT’D)**
You understand the neighborhood already. That’s good.

**DANIEL**
You make this sound bigger than a lift from an airport.

**MARCUS**
Only because you keep trying to make it smaller.

Daniel looks away.

**DANIEL**
If I say no?

Marcus studies him. Almost kind.

**MARCUS**
You won’t.

A long beat.

**DANIEL**
...What time?

**MARCUS**
Midnight.
He’ll text the location.
Just drive the route you’re given and keep both hands visible if anyone stops you.

Daniel looks up sharply.

**DANIEL**
Why would anyone stop me?

Marcus gives the smallest shrug.

**MARCUS**
Because life enjoys coincidence.
And because you look nervous when spoken to directly.

He pats Daniel once on the shoulder.

**MARCUS (CONT’D)**
Get some sleep.
You’re entering the part of this where fatigue becomes expensive.

---

## SCENE 9: THE DETECTIVES ARRIVE (11:00 PM)
**INT. CROSS HOUSE – ENTRY / LIVING ROOM – LATE NIGHT**
Daniel is pacing. Nina sits still, watching him unravel with growing precision.

Doorbell. Not neighbor time. Daniel opens the door.

DETECTIVE NATHAN COLE and DETECTIVE LENA MERCER stand on the porch. No aggression. Badges out. Calm.

**COLE**
Mr. Cross. Sorry for the hour.

**MERCER**
We do our best work when people stop rehearsing.

Daniel tries to smile.

**DANIEL**
Is there a problem?

**COLE**
Depends how attached you are to the word “problem.”

Nina steps into frame beside Daniel.

**NINA**
What can we do for you, Detectives?

Mercer takes Nina in quickly. Registers intelligence. Control.

**MERCER**
We’re following up on a vehicle seen near a warehouse incident on the south side.

**DANIEL**
I work logistics. My car’s near warehouses all the time.

**COLE**
That’s true.
Most cars, however, are less enthusiastic about bleach.

Daniel says nothing. Mercer softens her voice.

**MERCER**
We’re not accusing you of anything, Mr. Cross.
We’re just trying to understand why a man with an early start would spend four in the morning deep-cleaning a trunk.

**NINA**
You’ve been talking to Mavis.

**COLE**
We’ve been listening to Mavis.
Talking would imply consent.

That almost gets a laugh out of Nina. Almost.

**DANIEL**
I spilled something.

**COLE**
Fertilizer?

Daniel looks at him. Caught.

**MERCER**
That’s interesting.
Because your wife doesn’t look like a woman who buys fertilizer by the gallon.

Nina turns slightly toward Daniel. Tiny movement. Massive meaning.

**NINA**
Detective, are we helping you with something specific, or are you just inventorying our lies?

Mercer actually smiles at that.

**MERCER**
That depends.
Are you offering them in bulk?

Cole glances toward the driveway.

**COLE**
We’ll need to speak again tomorrow.
Try not to clean anything else tonight.
It makes the chronology insecure.

He turns to leave. Mercer lingers half a beat, to Nina—

**MERCER**
You seem like the organized one.
When this gets worse, I’d prefer you call before your husband improvises.

The detectives leave. Daniel closes the door and leans against it.

**DANIEL**
How bad is this?

Nina looks at him, devastated and furious.

**NINA**
That depends.
What exactly did you do before the neighbors brought bread?

---

## SCENE 10: THE PICKUP (MIDNIGHT)
**EXT. PRIVATE AIRFIELD ACCESS ROAD – MIDNIGHT**
Cold, empty, ugly light. Wind across asphalt. Daniel’s SUV idles alone.

He checks his phone. No new messages.

A side door opens from a low hangar building.

VIKTOR SARIC emerges carrying one heavy duffel. Dark coat. Cheap digital watch on the inside of his wrist. Exhaustion so complete it looks sculpted.

He gets into the passenger seat. Sets the bag carefully on the floor. Checks his watch.

Silence.

**DANIEL**
You’re... Marcus’s brother-in-law?

**VIKTOR**
No.

A beat.

**DANIEL**
Oh.

Silence. Daniel starts driving.

**DANIEL (CONT’D)**
So, uh... long flight?

**VIKTOR**
Daniel.

Daniel stops talking. VIKTOR checks the watch again.

**VIKTOR (CONT’D)**
I have not slept in two days.
Please do not make me know more about you.

Daniel swallows.

**DANIEL**
Right.

A few seconds of road noise.

**DANIEL (CONT’D)**
What’s in the bag?

Viktor looks at him. Too tired to fake courtesy.

**VIKTOR**
Something you should continue not asking about if you intend to see next week.

Daniel grips the wheel tighter.

**DANIEL**
Marcus said this was just a favor.

**VIKTOR**
Marcus is a hospitable man.
That does not make you safe.

A beat. VIKTOR checks the watch again.

**VIKTOR (CONT’D)**
Drive the speed limit.
If we’re pulled over, you say as little as possible and look exactly as stupid as you currently feel.

Daniel stares at the road.

**DANIEL**
I’m not a criminal.

**VIKTOR**
No.
That is what makes you dangerous.

[END OF PILOT]
