# NEXT DOOR — ENVIRONMENT GENERATION VARIABLES
**STATUS: LOCKED**
**PURPOSE: VIRELLE STUDIOS SET ANCHORING**

This document contains the exact generation variables for all 11 core locations. It must be used as the source of truth for environment generation prompts to prevent set drift and maintain the visual opposition between the two families.

---

## 1. CROSS FOYER
**Base Look:** A slightly cramped entry space that immediately reveals the family's organizational struggle.
**Palette:** Warm beige, scuffed oak, faded runner rug.
**Texture Keywords:** Cluttered, lived-in, scuffed, warm, practical.
**Lighting Mode:** 
- *Morning:* Bright but chaotic light spilling from the kitchen.
- *Night:* A single warm overhead light, casting soft shadows. Colder porch light pushes in when the door opens.
**Emotional Role:** The transition from the public world to private stress. Vulnerability.
**Power Role:** Invasion threshold. Where outside power enters domestic safety.
**Recurring Props:** A pile of unopened mail on a small console table, mismatched shoes near the door. The Welcome Basket.
**Continuity Risks:** The arrangement of the mail and shoes must remain consistent or purposefully altered by action.
**Negative Prompts:** Do not make it grand. No double doors. No marble floors. No perfectly arranged decor.
**Best Promo Angle:** A low angle looking up from the shoes to Daniel's exhausted face. Detectives framed by cold exterior light pushing into the warm foyer.
**Best Dialogue Angle:** Tight over-the-shoulder, emphasizing the lack of space.

## 2. CROSS LIVING ROOM
**Base Look:** A comfortable but slightly dated family room. Furniture that has been owned for ten years.
**Palette:** Terracotta, denim blue, worn leather.
**Texture Keywords:** Soft, worn, fabric-heavy, slightly faded.
**Lighting Mode:**
- *Day:* Filtered sunlight through slightly dusty blinds.
- *Night:* Warm lamp light, TV glow.
**Emotional Role:** The illusion of safety.
**Power Role:** The only place the family feels united, though the cracks are showing.
**Recurring Props:** A slightly worn throw blanket on the sofa, a coffee table with water rings.
**Continuity Risks:** The position of the throw blanket.
**Negative Prompts:** No modern minimalist furniture. No stark white walls. No matching sets.
**Best Promo Angle:** A wide shot showing the family isolated on different pieces of furniture.
**Best Dialogue Angle:** Seated, eye-level, emphasizing the worn textures.

## 3. CROSS KITCHEN
**Base Look:** The operational center of the house. Busy, functional, showing the strain of daily life.
**Palette:** Warm wood cabinets, slightly outdated laminate counters, stainless steel appliances that aren't top-tier.
**Texture Keywords:** Cluttered, functional, warm, slightly greasy.
**Lighting Mode:**
- *Morning:* Harsh, bright overhead fluorescent mixed with window light.
- *Night:* A single pendant light over the island, creating an interrogation-like pool of light.
**Emotional Role:** The pressure cooker. Where the truth comes out.
**Power Role:** Nina's domain, but easily compromised by Daniel's secrets.
**Recurring Props:** A corkboard with overlapping school notices, a coffee maker that looks heavily used.
**Continuity Risks:** The state of the corkboard and the dishes in the sink.
**Negative Prompts:** No massive marble islands. No pristine, empty counters. No high-end chef's appliances.
**Best Promo Angle:** A tight shot across the island during an argument, blurring the clutter in the foreground.
**Best Dialogue Angle:** Profile shots across the counter, emphasizing the physical barrier between characters.

## 4. CROSS DRIVEWAY / GARAGE
**Base Look:** A standard suburban driveway leading to a cluttered, practical garage.
**Palette:** Concrete gray, faded asphalt, the dull colors of stored boxes.
**Texture Keywords:** Concrete, cardboard, dust, oil stains.
**Lighting Mode:**
- *Pre-dawn:* Cold blue light exposing panic.
- *Day:* Harsh, flat exterior sunlight.
- *Night:* A single, motion-sensor floodlight that casts harsh, unforgiving shadows.
**Emotional Role:** The seam between the safe house and the dangerous outside world. Masculine hidden-failure zone.
**Power Role:** Daniel's first collapse zone. Evidence anxiety zone.
**Recurring Props:** Stacks of plastic storage bins, a slightly rusty lawnmower.
**Continuity Risks:** The exact placement of the storage bins where the briefcase is hidden.
**Negative Prompts:** No clean epoxy floors. No luxury cars. No organized tool walls.
**Best Promo Angle:** Daniel illuminated only by the trunk light in the cold pre-dawn driveway.
**Best Dialogue Angle:** Low angle, emphasizing the claustrophobia of the cluttered garage.

## 5. VALE ENTRY
**Base Look:** A massive, silent, perfectly composed space designed to intimidate guests under the guise of welcome.
**Palette:** Stark white, charcoal gray, brushed steel, polished marble.
**Texture Keywords:** Cold, hard, reflective, immaculate, silent.
**Lighting Mode:**
- *Day:* Diffused, perfect gallery lighting from high windows.
- *Night:* Architectural uplighting that makes the space feel like a museum.
**Emotional Role:** Intimidation and assessment. Makes guests feel small.
**Power Role:** First intimidation threshold. Social dominance zone. Where the Vales establish dominance before a guest even takes off their coat.
**Recurring Props:** A single, massive, perfectly arranged floral centerpiece on a glass table.
**Continuity Risks:** The floral arrangement must never look wilted or changed unless by a character.
**Negative Prompts:** No clutter. No shoes by the door. No mail. No warm colors.
**Best Promo Angle:** A wide, symmetrical shot emphasizing the scale of the space and the smallness of the guest.
**Best Dialogue Angle:** Wide shots that keep the characters small in the frame, emphasizing the environment's dominance.

## 6. VALE PATIO
**Base Look:** An immaculate outdoor entertaining space that feels more like a high-end resort than a backyard.
**Palette:** Slate gray, dark wood, manicured green.
**Texture Keywords:** Smooth stone, sharp edges, perfect foliage, sterile.
**Lighting Mode:**
- *Day:* Filtered sunlight through architectural pergolas.
- *Night:* Perfect, low-level landscape lighting. Fire pit glow.
**Emotional Role:** Seduction. Where the Vales show off their wealth to disarm targets.
**Power Role:** Where the Vales pretend to be relaxed while remaining entirely vigilant.
**Recurring Props:** High-end, untouched outdoor furniture. A perfectly clean fire pit.
**Continuity Risks:** The arrangement of the outdoor cushions.
**Negative Prompts:** No children's toys. No messy gardens. No plastic furniture.
**Best Promo Angle:** A slow push-in across the perfect lawn toward the fire pit.
**Best Dialogue Angle:** Over-the-shoulder shots with the fire pit glowing softly in the background.

## 7. VALE KITCHEN
**Base Look:** A massive, sterile, chef-grade kitchen that looks like it has never been used to cook a messy meal.
**Palette:** Matte black, white marble, stainless steel.
**Texture Keywords:** Seamless, reflective, cold, sharp.
**Lighting Mode:**
- *Day:* Bright, even, shadowless light.
- *Night:* Under-cabinet LEDs creating sharp, precise lines of light. Surgical lighting.
**Emotional Role:** The interrogation room. Surgery by hospitality. Quiet psychological combat.
**Power Role:** Celeste's absolute domain. Female chessboard zone. Where she isolates and dissects targets.
**Recurring Props:** A massive, completely empty marble island. An espresso machine that looks like a piece of lab equipment. Wine storage.
**Continuity Risks:** The absolute emptiness of the counters.
**Negative Prompts:** No clutter. No warm wood. No signs of actual cooking.
**Best Promo Angle:** Nina and Celeste separated by the massive Vale kitchen island under surgical lighting.
**Best Dialogue Angle:** Profile shots that highlight the sharp, clean lines of the background.

## 8. VALE GARAGE
**Base Look:** A massive, clinically clean space that looks more like a high-end showroom or an operating theater than a garage.
**Palette:** Bright white epoxy, brushed steel, matte black vehicles.
**Texture Keywords:** Glossy, sterile, industrial, silent.
**Lighting Mode:**
- *Night:* Blinding, shadowless overhead LED panels. Cold light spilling from the cracked door.
**Emotional Role:** The truth. Where the criminal reality is exposed. Practical, cold, organized, routinely criminal.
**Power Role:** Marcus's operational center. Viktor's truth space. Where the operation is revealed without performance.
**Recurring Props:** High-end black SUVs. Heavy, locked steel cabinets. Tools in order. Practical workbench.
**Continuity Risks:** The exact position of the vehicles.
**Negative Prompts:** No cardboard boxes. No scattered tools. No oil stains. No clutter whatsoever. Not a fantasy lair.
**Best Promo Angle:** Viktor's face half-lit by the cold light spilling from the cracked Vale garage door.
**Best Dialogue Angle:** Wide shots that emphasize the sterile, terrifying emptiness of the space.

## 9. MAVIS PORCH
**Base Look:** A meticulously maintained but slightly dated suburban porch. The watchtower.
**Palette:** Pastel yellow, white trim, dark green foliage.
**Texture Keywords:** Painted wood, stiff fabrics, manicured.
**Lighting Mode:**
- *Day:* Bright, direct sunlight.
- *Night:* A slightly too-bright porch light that illuminates the street. Shadow play.
**Emotional Role:** Surveillance and judgment. Alertness masked as leisure.
**Power Role:** Mavis's panopticon. Where she feels she controls the neighborhood.
**Recurring Props:** A pair of binoculars partially hidden by a potted plant. A perfectly clean welcome mat. Mavis's notebook and mug.
**Continuity Risks:** The position of the binoculars and notebook.
**Negative Prompts:** No peeling paint. No dead plants. No modern furniture.
**Best Promo Angle:** Mavis's silhouette behind lace curtains, a notebook on the sill.
**Best Dialogue Angle:** High angle looking down from the porch to the street, emphasizing Mavis's perceived superiority.

## 10. STREET CURB / BIN LINE
**Base Look:** The literal dividing line between the properties. A normal suburban street that feels increasingly exposed.
**Palette:** Asphalt gray, green lawns, the dull colors of trash bins.
**Texture Keywords:** Rough asphalt, manicured grass, plastic.
**Lighting Mode:**
- *Night:* The harsh, yellow-orange glow of sodium streetlights. Deep shadows between the pools of light.
**Emotional Role:** Exposure. Nowhere to hide.
**Power Role:** The neutral zone where the power dynamics are negotiated in public.
**Recurring Props:** The trash bins.
**Continuity Risks:** The exact alignment of the bins on the curb.
**Negative Prompts:** No bustling activity. The street must feel unnaturally empty and quiet.
**Best Promo Angle:** A low-angle shot down the center of the street, showing the two houses facing each other like fortresses.
**Best Dialogue Angle:** Profile shots with the opposing house looming in the deep background out of focus.

## 11. AIRFIELD ACCESS ROAD / TARMAC EDGE
**Base Look:** An industrial, desolate space far from the suburban safety. Chain-link fences and flat concrete.
**Palette:** Rust, concrete, warning-sign yellow, deep night sky.
**Texture Keywords:** Chain-link, cracked concrete, cold, industrial.
**Lighting Mode:**
- *Night:* Harsh, distant floodlights cutting through the darkness. Sweeping beams from security lights. Ugly sodium glare.
**Emotional Role:** Consequence. The terrifying scale of the Vales' operation. Isolation. Daniel is underqualified. Viktor is in his element.
**Power Role:** Absolute Vale dominance. The Cross family does not belong here. Criminal threshold.
**Recurring Props:** Unmarked cargo vans. Chain-link gates.
**Continuity Risks:** The position of the vans and the gates.
**Negative Prompts:** No suburban elements. No warmth. No safe spaces.
**Best Promo Angle:** A wide shot showing a small character silhouetted against the massive scale of a waiting aircraft or cargo convoy.
**Best Dialogue Angle:** Tight shots with harsh, directional lighting cutting across the characters' faces.
