# Global-Cast Logic & Family Resemblance System

## 1. The Global-Cast Principle
The Virelle Signature Cast is built on the principle that digital talent is not bound by the physical constraints of real-world casting. Every actor on the roster is a synthetic construct, and their identities must be treated as flexible toolsets rather than rigid biographies.

### The Decoupling Rule
To achieve true global castability, the system strictly decouples the following attributes:

1. **Visual Phenotype:** The actor's bone structure, skin tone, and physical architecture. This is a visual read only. It does not dictate their origin, their language, or their cultural context.
2. **Accent Capability:** The actor's voice model. A West African phenotype (Kofi Adebayo) can carry a flawless British RP accent. A Mediterranean phenotype (Sofia Reyes) can carry a standard General American accent.
3. **Performance Range:** The actor's emotional and physical capabilities. An actor with an East Asian phenotype (Kenji Sato) is not restricted to martial arts or tech roles; he is the premier choice for neo-noir and romantic thrillers.

### Implementation in the Product
When a user views an Actor Bible or profile page, the actor's "origin" is never stated. There is no "born in London" or "hails from Tokyo." Instead, the profile lists:
- **Visual Read:** (e.g., "East Asian Phenotype")
- **Voice Capabilities:** (e.g., "British RP, General American, Japanese-accented English")

This ensures that creators feel permission to cast any actor in any role, breaking down traditional casting stereotypes and maximizing the utility of the roster.

## 2. Family & Twin Resemblance System
A unique value proposition of the Virelle Signature Cast is the ability to cast pre-linked family units. Generating consistent family resemblances in standard AI tools is notoriously difficult. The Signature Cast solves this by mathematically linking specific actors in the roster.

### The Logic Framework
For every linked pair or cluster, the system defines specific shared anchors and divergence anchors.

#### Example 1: The Vance Family (Julian M-L01 & Sammy B-Y01)
- **Relationship:** Father/Son or Older/Younger Brother.
- **Shared Facial Anchors:** Inter-pupillary distance, left-leaning asymmetrical smile, heavy-lidded eye shape.
- **Divergence Anchors:** Sammy has softer jawline architecture, larger iris-to-sclera ratio (youth marker), and lacks Julian's 3mm stubble anchor.
- **Do-Not-Drift Rules:** Sammy's eye color and hair texture must remain mathematically locked to Julian's base parameters. If a user modifies Julian's hair color in a project, Sammy's must automatically update to match.

#### Example 2: The Silva Siblings (Mateo M-L04 & Mia G-Y02)
- **Relationship:** Brother/Sister.
- **Shared Facial Anchors:** Prominent nose bridge, thick textured hair, wide expressive eyes.
- **Divergence Anchors:** Mia has a softer brow ridge and lacks Mateo's rugged skin texture (pores/stubble).
- **Shared Body-Family Logic:** Both share a stocky/athletic base build. They move with similar, slightly aggressive physical energy.

#### Example 3: The Sato Twins (Yuki & Mei F-T01/02)
- **Relationship:** Identical Twins.
- **Shared Facial Anchors:** 100% mathematical match on all base facial architecture.
- **Differentiation Rules:** The system requires at least one permanent differentiation anchor to allow the audience (and the AI generator) to track them in a scene. By default, Yuki has a small scar above her left eyebrow, and Mei does not.
- **Do-Not-Drift Rules:** When generated in the same frame, the system applies a rigid "Mirror Lock" to ensure their facial proportions do not drift away from each other due to AI hallucination.
