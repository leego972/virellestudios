# NEXT DOOR: PILOT SCRIPT (PART 2)

## SCENE 4: MIA AND JADEN (4:00 PM)
**Scene Description:** The street curb. MIA CROSS is struggling to drag two exceptionally heavy garbage cans to the curb. JADEN VALE is sitting on the hood of his brother’s parked car across the street, watching her struggle. He doesn't offer to help.
**Emotional Objective:** Establish Mia's directness and Jaden's guarded, observant nature.
**Subtext:** Mia is frustrated by the heavy bags (which Daniel packed the night before). Jaden is assessing her as a variable.
**Performance Tone:** Teen awkwardness masking genuine intelligence. Flirtatious hostility.

**MIA**
(Dropping a bag, panting, glaring across the street)
You're not going to help. You're just going to watch.

**JADEN**
(Without looking up from his phone)
I’m on break.

**MIA**
From what? Sitting?

Jaden finally looks up. He takes in the sweat on her forehead, the sheer weight of the bag she’s dragging. He slides off the hood and walks over slowly.

**JADEN**
(Guarded, cool)
You guys throw away a lot of rocks or something? That thing looks like it weighs ninety pounds.

**MIA**
(Kicking the bag, frustrated)
I don't know. My dad packed them last night. He's on a cleaning tear.

Jaden reaches down, grabs the handle of the bag, and lifts it effortlessly into the bin. He doesn't brag about it. He just does it.

**JADEN**
(Understated)
He must have been cleaning the whole garage.

**MIA**
(Looking at him, a little defensive but curious)
Thanks. You're Jaden, right? The quiet one. Your mom came over this morning. She brought bread.

**JADEN**
(A small, dry smile)
Yeah. Sourdough. It’s a tactical strike. She uses carbs to soften up the neighbors.

**MIA**
(Laughs, surprised by his honesty)
Well, it worked. My dad ate half a loaf.

**JADEN**
(Looking at the Cross house, his eyes narrowing slightly)
Just tell him not to eat the crust. That’s where she hides the poison.

**What Changes:** Mia realizes Jaden is not a generic suburban teen; he is highly observant and quietly rebellious against his own family. Jaden realizes Mia is honest, but her father is hiding something heavy in the trash.

## SCENE 5: MAVIS INTRUDES (6:45 PM)
**Scene Description:** The Cross living room. Daniel and Nina are getting ready for the dinner at the Vales. Daniel is frantic, looking for his watch. The doorbell rings. Daniel freezes. Nina opens the door. It’s MAVIS WHITLOCK, holding a small Tupperware container.
**Emotional Objective:** Mavis weaponizes her age to invade their space and gather intel.
**Subtext:** Mavis is probing for weaknesses. Nina is trying to block her without being rude. Daniel is terrified she knows about the trunk.
**Performance Tone:** Socially invasive politeness. A comedic pressure cooker.

**MAVIS**
(Stepping inside before Nina can stop her)
Nina, dear. I brought you some leftover casserole. I know moving is a nightmare. I saw Daniel up at four this morning scrubbing his car, so I thought, 'Those poor people haven't had time to cook.'

Daniel appears in the hallway. He looks like he’s about to have a heart attack.

**DANIEL**
(Voice strained)
Mavis. Thank you. That’s very... observant of you.

**MAVIS**
(Smiling, handing the container to Nina)
Oh, I don't sleep much. At my age, sleep is just a rehearsal. You know, Daniel, I noticed you started backing into the driveway today.

**DANIEL**
(Swallowing hard)
Just... easier to pull out in the morning.

**MAVIS**
(Nodding slowly, eyes tracking every detail of his face)
Of course it is. Quicker getaway. My late husband used to back in when he owed money to the bookies in '82. He said it saved him three seconds. He was wrong, of course. They broke his legs anyway.

Nina steps smoothly between Mavis and Daniel, physically breaking the line of sight.

**NINA**
(Controlled, polite but firm)
Thank you for the casserole, Mavis. We’re actually just heading across the street for dinner. The Vales invited us.

**MAVIS**
(Her smile falters slightly; she adjusts her glasses)
The Vales. Oh. Well. Be careful over there, dear. Celeste’s wine is excellent, but her questions are very expensive.

**What Changes:** Mavis establishes herself as a constant, unpredictable threat. She has noticed the logistical failures (the car washing, the backing in) and forced Daniel to realize he is being watched constantly.

## SCENE 6: THE VALE DINNER (8:00 PM)
**Scene Description:** The Vale patio. Perfect lighting. Expensive wine. Marcus is pouring drinks. Celeste is directing the conversation. Daniel is trying to act normal. Nina is watching Celeste.
**Emotional Objective:** The Vales are testing the Cross family. The Crosses are trying to survive the interrogation.
**Subtext:** Every polite question is a probe for weakness.
**Performance Tone:** High-class tension. Criminal-coded politeness masking absolute ruthlessness.

**MARCUS**
(Pouring wine, precise and calm)
So, Daniel. Logistics. That’s a tough game right now. Supply chains are a mess. How are you holding up?

**DANIEL**
(Taking a sip, trying to sound relaxed)
It’s fine. We manage. You just have to... adapt. Find new routes.

**CELESTE**
(Soft-spoken, cutting)
Adaptability is so important. Marcus is in acquisitions. He says the hardest part isn't finding the asset, it’s making sure the current owner realizes they don't want it anymore.

Nina looks sharply at Celeste.

**NINA**
That sounds less like acquisitions and more like hostile takeovers.

**MARCUS**
(A dry, quiet laugh)
The line is very thin, Nina. It usually depends on who has the better lawyers.

**CELESTE**
(Turning to Daniel, her voice dripping with sympathy)
You look exhausted, Daniel. Truly. Are you sleeping at all? Moving is stressful, but you look like a man carrying the weight of the world.

**DANIEL**
(Gripping his wine glass too tightly)
I’m fine. Just... adjusting to the new neighborhood.

**MARCUS**
(Leaning forward slightly, the intimidation quiet but absolute)
It’s a very safe street, Daniel. We make sure of it. Nothing happens here without us knowing. If you ever have a problem... an unexpected debt, a logistical failure... you come to me. We protect our own.

**What Changes:** The dinner shifts from a neighborly welcome to a quiet, terrifying assertion of dominance. The Vales are not just wealthy neighbors; they are predators who smell blood in the water.
